The Highlights ✨

Ten days, a boat through a wine-country canyon, open-air concerts, and as little forced fun as possible.

Full Itinerary 🗓️

Tap a day to open it up.

17 MonArrival›
Lands Santiago (SCQ) 22:00, then the drive to A Coruña (45–60 min). Realistically at Pablo's close to midnight. Nothing planned for this evening, arrival and bed.
18 TueMOP Foundation + Semana Clásica: Orquesta GAOS›
Afternoon: MOP Foundation, Muelle de Batería. Current exhibition is Paolo Roversi's "Doubts" (20 June–20 Sept 2026), free entry, self-paced.

Evening: Orquesta GAOS, Plaza de María Pita, 21:00 (consistent with prior years, not yet confirmed for 2026).
📌 Free, no booking. Arrive 20–30 min early for a decent spot.
19 WedBeach day: Bastiagueiro / Ares›
Sheltered, calmer water than the city beaches. Low-key, no plans needed.
20 ThuFragas do Eume (TBC — possible Pablo pickup)›
Old-growth Atlantic forest, Caaveiro monastery.
⚠️ The access road is closed to cars 23 June – 11 Sept. Call ahead to check shuttle status: 604 926 357 / 981 080 585 / 981 080 300. Without a shuttle it's a long walk, not a stroll.
📌 TBC: Pablo's away the first few days (leaving a key). Happy to pick him up from Santiago if that's easier for him, otherwise no worries if he'd rather make his own way back. Just let us know either way and we'll fit around it, including moving Eume to another day if needed.
21 FriBeach (maybe) + Semana Clásica: Xove Orquestra Sinfónica›
Morning: beach (Bastiagueiro / Ares), pencilled in, not fixed.

Lunch at Pablo's, relaxed afternoon at home.

Evening: tapas around 7–8pm, then Xove Orquestra Sinfónica de Galicia, Plaza de María Pita, 21:00 (consistent with prior years, not yet confirmed for 2026).
📌 Free, no booking.
22 SatRibeira Sacra — Miño boat trip + lunch›
Full day, roughly 2–2.5 hrs each way. Switched to the Miño river route this year (Belesar embarcadero) rather than the Sil canyon, since Sil was already done last year, gentler, more vineyard-lined, passes Cabo do Mundo.

Plan: boat trip earlier in the day, then lunch at A Faragulla (Chantada), about 10 min drive from the embarcadero, around 2–3pm. Real kitchen, sit-down meal, strong wine list, not just a bodega tasting.
📌 Boat: reservas.rutasembalses.es, official Diputación de Lugo site, book well in advance, August sells out. Pick a morning departure to leave time for lunch after.
📌 A Faragulla lunch service runs 1:30–3:30pm every day, so this plan works regardless of which day you go, unlike dinner which is Fri/Sat only.
23 SunOpen day / Sobrado dos Monxes›
Working Cistercian monastery, quiet, short visit. Or just rest.
📌 Church entry free, no booking. Cloisters may have a small on-site fee.
24 MonLugo & Melide›
Walk the Roman wall circuit in Lugo, then lunch in Melide, some of the best pulpo on the Camino.
📌 Wall walk is free, no booking. Book a table in Melide if you have a specific pulpería in mind (A Garnacha, Ezequiel), August gets busy.
25 TueBeach day: Doniños / Frouxeira›
Wilder, more open than the city beaches. Dunes, more space, fewer people.
26 WedOurense (optional) + Judeline concert›
Optional full day: As Burgas natural hot springs, free, plus Ourense old town. Evening: Judeline, As Noites do Parque, Parque Santa Margarita.
📌 Hot springs free, no booking. Concert is ticketed via Ataquilla/Ticketmaster, on sale since March, check availability soon.
27 ThuDeparture›
Morning only. Flight is 16:50 from Santiago (SCQ), so allow the drive (45–60 min) plus check-in time, realistically leaving A Coruña by 14:00–14:30. Nothing scheduled beyond a relaxed morning.
